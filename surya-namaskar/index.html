Pranamasana
Prayer Pose
Alignment & Tips
  • Feet close together — weight evenly distributed across all four corners of both feet
  • Palms joined at Anahata chakra — thumbs lightly pressing the sternum, fingers pointing upward
  • Spine tall and vertical, shoulders drawn back and down — chest naturally open without forcing
  • Soften the gaze or close the eyes — breathe naturally and set your sankalpa (intention) for the practice
Stand at the top of your mat. Feet together, spine long. Exhale, bring palms together at the heart in Pranamasana.

Hasta Utthanasana
Raised Arms Pose
Alignment & Tips
  • Arms sweep up beside the ears — inner upper arms brushing or nearly touching the sides of the head
  • Backbend initiates from the thoracic spine and upper chest — the lumbar does not over-compress
  • Pelvis stays neutral — do not push the hips forward or allow the lower back to collapse into a deep arch
  • Gaze follows the hands upward and tilts slightly back, in line with the natural arc of the spine
Inhale, sweep the arms up beside the ears, gently arch back, lifting the chest — Hasta Utthanasana.

Padahastasana
Hand to Foot Pose
Alignment & Tips
  • Fold from the hip joints, not the waist — lead the movement with the chest and lengthen the front body as you descend
  • Palms flat on the floor or fingertips beside the feet — in the traditional Rishikesh sequence, the aim is palms fully grounded
  • Kneecaps draw up as the quadriceps engage — legs are straight but not hyper-extended; knees may soften if hamstrings are tight
  • Crown of the head releases toward the shins — neck completely relaxed, no tension in the throat or jaw
Exhale, fold from the hips, bring hands down beside the feet, head towards the knees — Padahastasana.

Ashwa Sanchalanasana
Equestrian Pose
Alignment & Tips
  • Right leg steps back as far as possible — right knee rests on the mat, top of the foot flat on the floor
  • Left knee is directly above the left ankle — shin vertical, forming approximately a 90° angle at the knee
  • Fingertips press into the floor on either side of the left foot — spine long, pelvis sinking toward the earth
  • Chest lifts and opens, spine extends, chin slightly raised — soft gaze forward and gently upward
Inhale, step the right leg back into a low lunge, right knee on the mat, chest open, look forward — Ashwa Sanchalanasana.

Parvatasana
Mountain Pose
Alignment & Tips
  • Left foot steps back to join the right — feet hip-width apart or together depending on practice tradition, heels pressing firmly toward the floor
  • Hips lift high toward the ceiling — body forms a clear inverted V, weight distributed evenly between hands and feet
  • Spine lengthens from tailbone to crown — back is flat rather than rounded; tailbone reaches upward and back
  • Head released between the upper arms, ears in line with the biceps — neck long and relaxed, no compression in the cervical spine
Exhale, step the left foot back, lift the hips up and back into an inverted V — Parvatasana.

Ashtanga Namaskara
Eight-Limbed Salutation
Alignment & Tips
  • Eight points contact the mat: two feet (toes tucked), two knees, two palms, chest, and chin — this is Ashtanga Namaskara, the prostration pose
  • Hips remain lifted off the floor — the body does not lie flat; the pelvis stays raised between the knees and the chest
  • Elbows draw in close to the ribcage, pointing straight back — forearms remain roughly vertical
  • Transition flows from Parvatasana by lowering knees first, then chest, then chin to the mat — movement is controlled, not a collapse
Hold the breath, lower knees, chest and chin to the mat, hips slightly lifted — Ashtanga Namaskara.

↑ Inhale
Bhujangasana
Cobra Pose
Alignment & Tips
  • Palms are flat on the mat beside the lower ribs — fingers spread, elbows hugging the sides of the torso
  • Chest slides forward along the mat before lifting — this gliding action protects the lumbar and opens the upper chest correctly
  • Elbows retain a visible bend — this is Bhujangasana (Cobra), not Urdhva Mukha Svanasana; the navel remains in contact with or close to the floor
  • Shoulders roll back and down, collarbones broad — no shrugging; the back muscles, not the arms, do the lifting work
Inhale, slide the chest forward, roll the shoulders back, lift the heart into Bhujangasana, elbows close to the body.

Parvatasana
Mountain Pose
Alignment & Tips
  • Toes tuck under, press firmly through the palms — exhale as the hips lift up and back into the inverted V
  • Exact mirror of Pose 5 — re-establish the same alignment: long flat spine, tailbone high, heels descending
  • Pause for a breath in the pose — feel the gentle traction in the spine as the sitting bones draw away from the hands
  • Heels press down toward the floor — this lengthens the hamstrings and calves progressively with each round
Exhale, tuck the toes, lift the hips back up into Parvatasana.

Ashwa Sanchalanasana
Equestrian Pose — other leg
Alignment & Tips
  • Right foot steps forward between the hands — foot arrives fully between the palms, not short; adjust if needed
  • Left knee rests on the mat, top of the left foot flat on the floor — direct mirror of Pose 4 on the opposite side
  • Right knee stays directly over the right ankle — do not allow the knee to drift inward or forward past the toes
  • Chest lifts, spine lengthens, gaze soft and forward — the same quality of open-hearted extension as the first lunge
Inhale, step the right foot forward between the hands, back knee down, chest open — Ashwa Sanchalanasana.

Padahastasana
Hand to Foot Pose
Alignment & Tips
  • Left foot steps forward to meet the right — feet together, weight evenly balanced, hands remain on the floor
  • Direct mirror of Pose 3 — re-establish the same Padahastasana: hip-fold, long spine, quadriceps engaged

Exhale, bring the back foot forward, feet together, fold into Padahastasana, head towards the knees.

Hasta Utthanasana
Raised Arms Pose
Alignment & Tips
  • Rise with a long, flat back — arms sweep forward and up as the torso ascends, maintaining length throughout the spine
  • Direct mirror of Pose 2 — re-enter the same gentle upper-thoracic backbend, arms beside the ears
  • Feel the full elongation of the body from the soles of the feet to the tips of the fingers at the top of the movement
  • Pelvis remains neutral at the crest of the pose — avoid over-arching the lumbar; the lift comes from the thoracic and chest
Inhale, press the feet, rise up with a long spine, sweep arms up, gentle backbend — Hasta Utthanasana.

Pranamasana
Prayer Pose — Return
Alignment & Tips
  • Exhale as the palms draw back to Anjali Mudra at the heart — one complete half-round is finished
  • Stand completely still for a full breath — observe the warmth, the heartbeat, and any shift in energy (prana)
  • This completes one half-cycle; repeat the entire sequence leading with the left leg in Poses 4 and 9 to complete one full round
  • Pranamasana — bow inward with awareness before beginning the next round or closing the practice
Exhale, bring the palms back to the heart center, return to Pranamasana.
