Unity is a practice

Eight movements of good living.

Universal principles, drawn from many lineages and shaped by lived experience. Not steps to climb. Movements that fold into each other — each one practiced quietly until the next becomes possible.

i

Relationship

How we meet others, the world, and ourselves. The first ground.

ii

Discipline

The quiet daily yes. What we return to without needing to be seen.

iii

Embodiment

The body as the instrument. Posture, gesture, the felt sense of being here.

iv

Breath

The bridge between inner and outer. The rhythm beneath every action.

v

Attention

What we choose to give to. The currency of an examined life.

vi

Focus

Holding one thing long enough for it to open.

vii

Meditation

Sustained presence. The practice that does itself.

viii

Unity

The remembering that nothing was ever separate.

CommonUnity cultivates these as core human skills — and uses the latest technology in a way that strengthens, rather than substitutes for, the human core.
