# The Adapted 8 Limbs

Status: v0.1. This document defines CommonUnity's adapted reading of Patanjali's eight limbs of yoga (ashtanga) as a **decision-making and creation-audit framework**. It is the operational backbone of [`../governance/audit-rituals.md`](../governance/audit-rituals.md).

The classical eight limbs are: *yama, niyama, asana, pranayama, pratyahara, dharana, dhyana, samadhi.* In CommonUnity, they are reread as eight conditions a piece of work must satisfy to be considered whole. Each limb is both a question (for decision-making before building) and a test (for auditing after building).

## 1. Yama — relational integrity

**Classical:** restraint toward others; non-harming, truthfulness, non-stealing, energetic continence, non-grasping.

**In CommonUnity:** does this work respect the sovereignty of the member and the integrity of the field?

- Does it harm or coerce anyone (members, facilitators, contributors)?
- Is it truthful in what it claims to do?
- Does it appropriate language, geometry, or practice without crediting source?
- Does it overreach what was actually asked for?

**Audit prompt:** *What relationships does this work touch, and does it leave each of them in greater integrity?*

## 2. Niyama — internal discipline

**Classical:** observances of self; purity, contentment, discipline, self-study, surrender.

**In CommonUnity:** is this work clean, well-kept, and built with care?

- Is the code/document clear and uncluttered?
- Have we resisted unnecessary scope creep?
- Have we acknowledged what we do not yet know?
- Did we surrender to the seed pattern rather than impose a clever idea?

**Audit prompt:** *Could a future steward pick this up cold and feel its care?*

## 3. Asana — structural seat

**Classical:** steady, sustainable posture.

**In CommonUnity:** does this work sit in a stable architectural place?

- Does it belong to the right layer (Compass / Om Cipher / Living Profile / Studio / cOMmons)?
- Is it built on the canonical engine where one exists, or does it duplicate logic?
- Will it still be sound under load, growth, or refactor?

**Audit prompt:** *Where does this sit in the system map, and is the seat stable?* (See [`../architecture/system-map.md`](../architecture/system-map.md).)

## 4. Pranayama — breath / energy flow

**Classical:** regulation of breath and life-force.

**In CommonUnity:** does energy flow cleanly through the work?

- Latency, responsiveness, render performance.
- For embodied inputs (Bhramari capture): is the somatic loop intact and felt?
- For data: does information move without obstruction or unnecessary friction?
- For the member journey: does the sequence (Invitation → Compass → Cipher/Profile → Studio → Personal Home → cOMmons) breathe naturally at this point?

**Audit prompt:** *Where does breath catch in this experience?*

## 5. Pratyahara — withdrawal of senses, removal of noise

**Classical:** withdrawing attention from external stimuli to settle inward.

**In CommonUnity:** has the work been stripped of noise?

- No vanity features, no unjustified ornament, no neon, no decorative AI text.
- No premature abstractions, no half-finished implementations.
- The visual language stays in warm dark-field discipline.
- The copy stays in the member's register, not the system's marketing voice.

**Audit prompt:** *What can be removed without loss? Remove it.*

## 6. Dharana — focused intention

**Classical:** single-pointed concentration.

**In CommonUnity:** does this work have one clear purpose?

- Can it be described in one sentence?
- Does every part of it serve that one purpose?
- If a member encountered only this surface, would they receive a coherent gesture?

**Audit prompt:** *State the one thing this does. Does everything in it serve that one thing?*

## 7. Dhyana — sustained meditation, coherence over time

**Classical:** continuous flow of attention.

**In CommonUnity:** does this work hold coherence across time and across surfaces?

- The Om Cipher rendered in Studio is identical to the one in cOMmons and the Personal Home.
- The member's Living Profile evolves but remains recognisably theirs.
- Decisions made in one milestone are honored in the next, or explicitly revisited in the [`decision-log`](../governance/decision-log.md).

**Audit prompt:** *Will this still be coherent three milestones from now?*

## 8. Samadhi — unity, integration with the whole

**Classical:** absorption, union.

**In CommonUnity:** does this work serve Unity — the core promise?

- Does it raise the baseline of creation toward Unity?
- Does it help a person translate source pattern into lived creation, public expression, and relational unity?
- Or is it merely capability that does not serve the promise?

**Audit prompt:** *Set the work down. Sit with the whole system. Is Unity served, or only function added?*

## How to use the 8 limbs

There are two modes.

### Before building: decision-making framework

When proposing a feature, scope change, or new surface, walk the eight limbs in order. The first limb that returns *no* is where the work needs to be reshaped. Do not begin until each limb returns at least *not yet, but the path is clear*.

### After building: creation-audit framework

At each milestone (see [`../governance/milestone-checklist.md`](../governance/milestone-checklist.md)), walk the eight limbs against the completed work. Record the audit in [`../governance/decision-log.md`](../governance/decision-log.md) using the template in [`../governance/integrity-review-template.md`](../governance/integrity-review-template.md).

A milestone is not complete until all eight limbs pass or any failures are explicitly accepted with a reason and a follow-up.
