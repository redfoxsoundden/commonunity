# OM Field

Status: v0.1 stub. Canonical source: [`commonunity-architecture-v0.2.md`](./commonunity-architecture-v0.2.md), section *"OM Field and the torus threshold"*.

## What the OM Field is

The OM Field is the underlying relational medium of CommonUnity. It is not a separate destination but a **threshold condition** through which movement happens between layers.

The floating torus threshold in Studio is the first visible manifestation of the OM Field. It already functions as an access ritual for opening the rooms of Studio. The same threshold logic extends outward into cOMmons and inward into the member's own practice.

## What the threshold can host

- The member's recorded Om / Bhramari tone.
- A fresh centering practice before entering Studio.
- A way to refresh or update the frequency signature.
- A path into the member's Studio.
- A future path into cOMmons or other Field spaces.

## Design stance

- The OM Field should not necessarily become a separate destination. It is the *passage*, not a *room*.
- The torus is not decoration — it is the visible form of the threshold condition.
- Movement through the threshold is always accompanied by a brief somatic act (tone, breath, attention).

- What is the minimum somatic act required at each crossing? (Likely: nothing in v0; an explicit tone-and-breath gesture by v1.)
