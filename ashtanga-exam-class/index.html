Three OMs · Meditation
Three OMs — Opening Meditation
Hand Placement — Añjali Mudrā
Bring the palms together at the heart centre (Anahata Chakra). Fingers point upward, thumbs rest lightly against the sternum. Allow the hands to touch but not press. Soften the shoulders, close the eyes, and relax the face.
The Three OMs
Take a full breath in through the nose. On the exhale, chant OM — feeling the vibration begin in the belly, rise through the chest, and dissolve at the crown. Let it fade completely into silence. Pause in that stillness before the next inhale. Repeat three times, each round going a little deeper.
Teacher Cue — Markus
"Bring your hands together at your heart centre. Feel the thumbs rest against your sternum. Close your eyes. Take a full breath in… and together: OMmmm… Let it fade completely. Rest in the silence. Again…"

Asana 1 of 15
Ammo
Asana 1
Padangusthasana
Big Toe Pose
  • Stand with feet hip-width apart, big toes parallel
  • Hinge at the hips, keeping the spine long as you fold
  • Wrap the first two fingers around each big toe
  • On each inhale lengthen the spine; on each exhale deepen the fold
  • Keep the legs straight but not locked — micro-bend if needed
Teacher Cue — Ammo
"Press the feet into the earth, lift the kneecaps, and fold from the hip crease — not the lower back. Let gravity do the work. Five breaths."
Asana 2 of 15
Ammo
Asana 2
Padahasthasana
Hand Under Foot Pose
  • From Padangusthasana — release the toes and slide both hands under the feet, palms up
  • Toes touch the wrist crease; weight forward on the balls of the feet
  • Keep knees soft or straight — work at your own edge
  • Crown of the head releases toward the earth
  • Five steady Ujjayi breaths
Teacher Cue — Ammo
"Slide the hands under the feet, toes touching the wrist. Allow the weight to shift slightly forward. Breathe length into the spine with every inhale."
Asana 3 of 15
Thanusha
Asana 3
Utthita Trikonasana
Extended Triangle Pose
  • Wide-legged stance — feet about 1 metre apart
  • Right foot turns out 90°, left foot turns in slightly
  • Extend the torso over the right leg; hand to shin, floor, or block
  • Left arm reaches straight up — both arms form one line
  • Gaze to the upper hand (Hasta Drishti) or forward if neck is sensitive
  • Repeat on the left side
Teacher Cue — Thanusha
"Lengthen both sides of the waist equally. Root down through the outer edge of the back foot. Reach long through the fingertips before you lower."
Asana 4 of 15
Thanusha
Asana 4
Prasarita Padottanasana
Wide-Legged Standing Forward Fold
Variations A · B · C · D
  • A — Hands on the floor shoulder-width; crown of head toward the earth
  • B — Hands on hips; fold deeply, spine long, crown drops
  • C — Fingers interlaced behind the back; arms arc overhead as you fold
  • D — Index and middle finger wrap big toes; elbows widen; deep fold
  • Feet parallel throughout; inner arches lift
Teacher Cue — Thanusha
"Each variation deepens the surrender. Let the crown of the head be heavy. Press the outer edges of the feet into the mat and breathe space into the hamstrings."
Asana 5 of 15

Asana 5
Parsvottanasana
Intense Side Stretch / Pyramid Pose
  • Step one foot forward — feet staggered about 1 metre apart
  • Square the hips toward the front foot
  • Hands in Paschima Namaskar (reverse prayer) or hold elbows behind the back
  • Fold forward over the front leg, spine long
  • Keep the back heel grounded; front foot parallel
  • Repeat on the left side
Teacher Cue — Ammo
"Square both hips before folding. Lengthen the back of the front leg. Think of it as a standing split forward — precise and grounded."
Asana 6 of 15

Asana 6
Ardha Baddha Padmottanasana
Half Bound Lotus Forward Fold
  • Place the right foot in half lotus on the left thigh
  • Bind the right arm behind the back to hold the right big toe
  • Ground down through the standing left foot
  • Fold forward, left hand to the floor or shin
  • Keep the standing knee soft; gaze at the nose (Nasagrai Drishti)
  • Repeat on the left side
Teacher Cue — Markus
"This is a balance and hip opener combined. Find your stillness before you fold. If the lotus is not available, a simple tree leg variation is fine."
Transition
Markus
Sun Salutation C · Transition
Surya Namaskar C
18 Vinyasa · Bridge to the seated sequence
Samasthiti — Stand at the top of the mat. Feet together, Ujjayi breath established. Set your intention.

Ekam — Inhale — Sweep the arms overhead, palms join, gentle upper backbend. Gaze to the thumbs.

Dve — Exhale — Swan dive forward and down into Uttanasana. Hands to the floor, crown releases.

Trīṇi — Inhale — Half lift: Ardha Uttanasana. Spine long and flat, chest draws forward.

Catvāri — Exhale — Step or jump back, lower to Chaturanga Dandasana. Body in one line, elbows 90°.

Pañca — Inhale — Roll over the toes into Urdhva Mukha Svanasana. Chest open, thighs off the floor.

Ṣaṭ — Exhale — Lift the hips back to Adho Mukha Svanasana. Take one breath here.

Sapta — Exhale / Aṣṭau — Inhale — Step the right foot forward, rise into Virabhadrasana I. Back heel grounds, hips square, arms overhead. Hold 5 breaths.

Continuing — Exhale, jump back to Chaturanga → Inhale, Upward Dog → Exhale, Downward Dog. Pivot to face the back of the mat.

Virabhadrasana I — Left — Step left foot forward, rise up. Arms overhead, front knee over ankle. Hold 5 breaths.

Open to Virabhadrasana II — Open the arms wide, hips rotate to the side. Left leg forward. Hold 5 breaths.

Pivot — Virabhadrasana II Right — Pivot 180° to face the front. Right leg forward, arms wide. Hold 5 breaths.

Exhale — Jump back to Chaturanga → Inhale, Upward Dog → Exhale, Downward Dog. Prepare for jump-through.

Jump-Through — From Downward Dog, bend the knees, engage Mula and Uddiyana Bandha. Swing both legs forward between the hands, cross-legged or straight through, and land seated without touching the floor.
Dandasana — Staff Pose
Land seated with both legs extended. Sit tall — spine vertical, crown lifting. Feet flexed, inner thighs pressing down, hands flat beside the hips. Gaze to the nose (Nasagrai Drishti). Take 5 steady Ujjayi breaths. This is the foundation for the seated sequence.
Teacher Cue — Markus
"After Ardha Baddha Padmottanasana, we move into Sun Salutation C — 18 vinyasa. This bridges us from standing all the way down to the floor. Meet me in Dandasana and we continue with the seated sequence."
Asana 7 of 15

Asana 7
Paschimottanasana
Seated Forward Fold
Variations A · B · C
  • A — Index and middle fingers hook around big toes; elbows lift and widen on the exhale
  • B — Hands hold the outer edges of the feet; chin moves toward shins
  • C — Hands clasped around the feet; deep forward fold, chest to thighs
  • Begin from Dandasana; hinge forward from the hips, not the waist
  • Five breaths in each variation; gaze at the feet (Padhayoragrai Drishti)
Teacher Cue — Markus
"Lead from the heart, not the head. On each exhale, allow the belly to soften toward the thighs. Let the breath be your teacher here."
Asana 8 of 15
Markus
Asana 8
Purvattanasana
Upward Plank / Reverse Tabletop Pose
  • From Dandasana — hands behind hips, fingers pointing forward
  • On inhale, press into the hands and feet, lift the hips to the sky
  • Point the toes; feet together or hip-width apart
  • Head can release back gently if the neck is comfortable
  • Engage the glutes and core; avoid collapsing into the lower back
  • Five breaths; gaze at the third eye (Broomadhye Drishti)
Teacher Cue — Markus
"This is the counter-pose to Paschimottanasana. Open the front body. Feel the chest expand and the heart lift toward the sky."
Asana 9 of 15
Thanusha
Asana 9
Urdhva Dhanurasana
Upward Bow / Wheel Pose
  • Lie on the back — feet hip-width, knees above ankles
  • Hands beside the ears, fingers pointing toward the shoulders
  • First press up onto the crown; then fully straighten the arms
  • Press the feet firmly down; inner thighs parallel
  • Three rounds; rest in Bridge or Knees-to-Chest between rounds
Counter Pose — Paschimattanasana
Follow immediately with a seated forward fold — 8–10 slow breaths. Allow the spine to decompress and release after the back-bend. Soften the belly; breathe into the back body.
Teacher Cue — Thanusha
"A full back-bend — prepare the body with care. Wheel or Bridge are both valid. After each round, come down slowly and draw the knees to the chest."
Counter-Pose
Thanusha
Counter-Pose
Paścimattānāsana
Seated Forward Fold
  • From lying down — sit up into Dandasana; legs extended, spine tall
  • Inhale: lengthen the spine; exhale: fold forward from the hips
  • Hold the big toes, outer feet, or place hands on the shins
  • Allow the spine to round naturally; release the back of the neck
  • Breathe deeply into the back body — 8 to 10 slow Ujjayi breaths
  • Each exhale: soften the belly and release a little deeper
Teacher Cue — Thanusha
"After the intensity of Urdhva Dhanurasana the spine needs to decompress. Don't rush — let the body melt forward with each exhale. This is not a stretch; it is a surrender. Stay for a full 8–10 breaths before we continue."
Notes
Paschimattanasana is the prescribed counter-pose in Ashtanga Vinyasa after any deep back-bend. It neutralises the lumbar spine and calms the nervous system before continuing to the closing sequence.
Asana 10 of 15
Ammo
Asana 10
Salamba Sarvangasana
Supported Shoulderstand
  • From lying down — roll the legs overhead and support the back with both hands
  • Elbows shoulder-width on the mat; hands spread wide on the back
  • Body is vertical — hips stacked over shoulders, feet over hips
  • Chin presses gently into the chest (Jalandhara Bandha)
  • Gaze at the big toes; hold for 10–25 breaths
  • Never turn the head while in this pose
Teacher Cue — Ammo
"Walk the hands toward the shoulder blades for more support. Engage Mula and Uddiyana Bandha. Feel the cooling, calming effect of the inversion."
Asana 11 of 15
Ammo
Asana 11
Halasana
Plough Pose
  • From Sarvangasana — lower the feet to the floor overhead
  • Legs straight; toes flex toward the floor if they don't reach
  • Interlace the fingers on the mat behind the back; roll the shoulders under
  • Continue the chin-lock; gaze at the navel (Nabi Drishti)
  • Hold for 8–10 breaths; breathe into the back body
Teacher Cue — Ammo
"Keep the back of the neck long — do not press the chin aggressively. Halasana stretches the entire posterior chain. Breathe slowly and deeply."
Asana 12 of 15
Markus
Asana 12
Karnapidasana
Ear Pressure Pose
  • From Halasana — bend the knees and lower them toward the ears
  • Knees press gently against or beside the ears
  • Arms can remain interlaced on the floor or wrap around the knees
  • The body forms a tight, compact shape — chin toward chest
  • Gaze at the navel; hold for 8 breaths
  • Stimulates the parasympathetic nervous system — deeply inward
Teacher Cue — Markus
"Allow the external world to go quiet. Ears covered, breath slow, body folded. Just listen to your own heartbeat."
Asana 13 of 15

Asana 13
Urdhva Padmasana
Upward Lotus Pose
  • From Sarvangasana — cross the legs into Padmasana (full lotus) while inverted
  • Hands support the knees or hold the lotus from below
  • Keep the spine vertical and the hips over the shoulders
  • Gaze at the big toes or close the eyes
  • Hold for 8–10 breaths; then slowly lower down
  • If lotus is unavailable, keep legs straight or use half-lotus
Teacher Cue — Markus
"Bring the lotus into the sky. Take your time coming down — roll the spine slowly, vertebra by vertebra."
Asana 14 of 15

Asana 14
Matsyasana
Fish Pose
  • Lie on the back — legs extended, hands tucked under the hips, palms down
  • Press the elbows into the mat and arch the upper back
  • Crown of the head rests lightly on the floor; chest lifts high
  • Legs are active — feet together, toes pointing
  • Gaze at the third eye; hold for 5–8 breaths
  • Counter-pose to Sarvangasana and Halasana — opens the throat and chest
Teacher Cue — Thanusha
"The weight is on the elbows, not the head. Lift the chest as high as you can. Breathe into the front of the throat — this is the counter to the inversions."
Asana 15 of 15
Thanusha
Asana 15
Uttana Padasana
Raised Leg Pose
  • From Matsyasana — keep the upper back arched and chest lifted
  • Raise both legs to approximately 45° from the floor
  • Arms extend alongside the legs, pointing toward the feet
  • Lower back stays connected to the mat; core engaged
  • Gaze at the toes; hold for 5–8 breaths
  • Release slowly — lower the legs and head together on an exhale
Teacher Cue — Thanusha
"Keep the chest open and the back arched as you raise the legs. This is a powerful core and chest opener combined. Breathe fully into the ribcage."
Closing
Markus
Closing
Śavāsana
Corpse Pose
  • Come to lie flat on your back; feet hip-width, let them fall open
  • Arms rest slightly away from the body, palms facing up
  • Close the eyes; release all effort — breath becomes natural
  • Body completely still; allow the nervous system to integrate
  • Hold for 5–7 minutes — longer if the practice was intense
Teacher Cue — Markus
"Come to lie on your back. Let the feet fall open, arms resting beside the body, palms facing up. Close the eyes. There is nothing to do. Simply be. Allow the body to fully absorb the practice."
Notes
Śavāsana is the most important posture of the practice. The integration that happens here is not optional — it is the harvest of everything that came before. Stay for a minimum of 5 minutes.
